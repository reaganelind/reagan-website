Research & Sources

❤️ Why Use Heartrate to Measure Acute Stress

Heart rate (HR) can be considered a valid measure of Sympathetic Nervous System activation—specifically at the cardiac level—because it shows a strong, direct relationship with physiological markers of sympathetic activity in the heart. It is the least invasive and most accessible measure of stress to the general population.

▪️ Why Square Breathing Helps Reduce Stress

Slow, controlled breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which lowers heart rate, reduces blood pressure, and decreases stress hormone (cortisol) levels. This helps shift the body from a “fight or flight” state into a calmer “rest and digest” state.

Breathing at a steady rhythm (around 4–6 breaths per minute) improves vagal tone, which is associated with better emotional regulation and reduced anxiety symptoms.

🏃‍♀️‍➡️ Why Physical Activity Helps Reduce Stress

Exercise can quickly reduce stress by lowering your body’s stress hormone (cortisol), decreasing blood pressure, and helping shift you out of the Sympathetic Nervous System. It also improves your mood and helps you feel more relaxed, making it an easy way to reset and return to a calmer state.

🌿 Why Ambient Sound Helps Reduce Stress

Soft ambient sounds such as rain, ocean waves, or wind help mask unpredictable environmental noise, reducing cognitive load on the brain.

This stable auditory environment can reduce sympathetic nervous system activity and support relaxation. It also helps the brain maintain a consistent focus state, which is why ambient sound is commonly used in meditation and sleep therapy.

🎨 Why Soft Background Colors Help Relaxation

Soft, low-contrast colors such as light blues, greens, and desaturated tones are associated with calmness and safety. These colors reduce visual stimulation and help lower cognitive arousal.

Research in environmental psychology suggests that calming color palettes can reduce perceived stress and support parasympathetic activation, especially when combined with controlled breathing.

📓 Why Journaling Helps Reduce Stress

Journaling helps offload thoughts from working memory, reducing mental overload and allowing the brain to process emotions more clearly.

Expressive writing has been shown to reduce anxiety, improve emotional regulation, and help individuals reframe stressful experiences in a more constructive way.

In the context of elevated heart rate or anxiety, journaling supports reflection, awareness of triggers, and long-term behavior change.
